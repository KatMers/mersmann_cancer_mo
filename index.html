Cervical Cancer Rates in Missouri by County

By Katy Mersmann
Published March 12, 2015
The Missouri Department of Health and Senior Services tracks incidences of cervical cancer in the state, broken out by county. In Missouri, the rate of cervical cancer is 8.3 percent, and the disease has a 2.6 percent death rate, statewide. In the southeast part of the state, rates of cervical cancer are higher overall, with a number of possible responsible factors.

Cervical cancer is the fourth most common cancer in women worldwide, and is caused by the human papillomavirus, according to the World Health Institute. The virus can cause changes in cervical cancer cells that can eventually develop into cancer, especially when the virus is not detected early.
